David Lawrence

David is a fourteen-year-old programmer, student, and mathematician residing in Clinton, Utah. He enjoys spending his downtime indoors and solving calculus problems, particularly ones dealing with integral calculus. When not feeling so productively inclined, David prefers to sit down and play some Minecraft or other such games. He is a fairly average builder and can develop fairly intricate redstone contraptions if given enough time. Other activities he enjoys include biking, reading, talking, and filming.

Time at Clinton Elementary (2012 - 2019)

Awards

While David's elementary education was fairly standard, there were a few notable details worth mentioning. David got the 'Hope of America' award in 6th Grade, has received the 'Student of the Month' award many times, and also got to eat lunch with the mayor of Clinton on two occasions. Student Enrichment Model

Throughout his education, David always has been at least a few steps ahead of his peers. This is normal, but it came to the point in 1st Grade where something had to be done. He was placed into a program called the Student Enrichment Model (SEM) along with another unnamed individual two years younger than usual. David continued to be in SEM for the remainder of his time at Clinton Elementary.

Being a Sunset Sundevil (2019 - 2022)

Awards

At Sunset Jr. High, David has obtained many awards. In the ten terms (2.5 years) he has been there, he has gotten a 4.0 GPA every time. Accelerated Math

Throughout David's education, mathematics have always been a strong suit for him. When being put into a middle school setting, the counselors found it fit to place him into an accelerated math course. Originally, this was meant to teach 7th Grade Math in a semester, then use the rest for 8th Grade Math; however, this group of students was different. When tested at the beginning of the year, the instructor found it clear that most students did not need that first half. As a result, he skipped it and simply taught David's class at an 8th Grade Level for the entire year.


The following year, instead of being placed in another accelerated class, David was thrown into a typical Sec. Math 1 classroom consisting of mostly ninth graders. While initially daunting, this environment became something he looked forward to, and he even was able to help teach his peers. After that, however, it was impossible to put him into a regular 10th Grade classroom. It was back to the old approach of an accelerated course. This portion has been roughly the same experience as the previous years as of yet.


Robotics Club

About a quarter of the way through David's 7th Grade year, a new club using VEX Robotics was announced. Instantly, he was hooked. During SEM, he had been introduced to a programming platform called Scratch, and he loved using it for simple and complex problems. These robots run off a very similar language, so he would be a fair bit ahead of his friends. At the end of the year, a robot he helped create went to a competition, and he continued the club the following year. David discontinued his membership to the club in his 9th Grade Year because he wanted to focus more on self-improvement.

Plans for the Future

NUAMES

Seeing as David has been ahead for so long, it would not make much sense to send him to a normal high school. As a compromise, his family intends to give him an education at the Northern Utah Academy for Math, Engineering, and Science (NUAMES) in Layton, Utah. Here, he hopes ot receive the education required for his future career and hobbies.


A Career

At the minute, David hopes to go into the field of computer science to become an ethical hacker. Not only will this provide a great salary (average in US: $101,165) to raise a family with, but also keep society safe and secure. Without these ethical hackers, the world would only learn from malicious actors that would not explain their methods.


If this plan fails, David has a backup. With his background in programming, he could easily do a bit more training and become a professional software or web developer. One of his childhood dreams was to become either a game tester or developer, and this passion never really went away. If all else fails, he could always go into education and become a mathematics teacher. This is a huge Plan C due to the salary, but he does see it as an option.
